Help / FAQ

What is HubProfit?

HubProfit is a simple, self-hosted web app that helps Amazon Hub Delivery drivers track their true net profit — not just gross earnings.

It starts with the standard $1.65-per-package pay rate and subtracts your real costs (fuel, vehicle wear, insurance, phone, and driver pay) so you always know what you actually made after expenses.

Your data lives entirely on your own machine. No account, no cloud, no subscription.

How does the $1.65/package earnings calculation work?

Amazon Hub Delivery pays a flat rate per package delivered. The default is $1.65, but you can change it in Settings → Pay per package.

Your gross earnings for any given day = packages delivered × pay per package. HubProfit then subtracts all your enabled expense categories to arrive at net profit.

How is the fuel cost estimated?

Fuel cost = miles driven ÷ MPG × gas price per gallon.

When you log a day, enter the miles driven for that delivery run — use your vehicle's trip odometer or a mileage-tracking app (MileIQ, Everlance, etc.) before and after each delivery. HubProfit does not track your GPS; you provide the miles.

Use the vehicle picker in Settings to auto-fill your MPG from the U.S. Department of Energy database, and keep the gas price updated to stay accurate.

What does "frozen rate" mean? Will changing Settings affect past entries?

No. Each day you log is saved with a snapshot of your current pay rate, MPG, gas price, and expense settings at the moment you hit Save.

If you change the pay rate from $1.65 to $1.80 next month, only days logged after that change use the new rate. Every past day keeps the exact numbers it was calculated with. This makes your historical records accurate and trustworthy.

How are monthly fixed costs (insurance, phone) spread across days?

Fixed monthly costs are divided evenly across the number of distinct days you worked that month.

Example: if insurance is $120/month and you worked 15 days in June, each day is charged $8.00. If you only worked 10 days, each day is charged $12.00. This reflects the true cost of running your business on the days you actually drove.

If multiple drivers share the same calendar date, that day's fixed-cost share is split evenly among them so the date is never charged more than once total.

How do I set up the vehicle to get my MPG automatically?

Go to Settings → Vehicle & Fuel. Select your vehicle's Year, then wait for the Make dropdown to load (it fetches real data from the U.S. DOE FuelEconomy.gov database), then select Model. Click Look up MPG and the combined city/highway MPG is filled in for you.

After saving, HubProfit uses that MPG for all future entries.

What is multi-driver mode?

If you run multiple drivers under your Hub account, enable multi-driver mode in Settings. A Driver dropdown will appear on the Log Day page so you can assign each entry to a specific driver.

Driver pay (a per-day expense) is only charged on days where a specific driver is assigned — days you drove yourself are not charged driver pay.

You can leave this off if you drive solo.

Can I export my data?

Yes. On the History page, click Download CSV to export all your entries (for the currently selected period) as a spreadsheet-compatible CSV file with date, packages, miles, hours, earnings, expenses, net, and hourly rate.

Is this free? Does my data leave my computer?

HubProfit is completely free and self-hosted. The app runs on your own machine and all data is stored locally in a SQLite database. Nothing is ever sent to an external server except the optional MPG lookup, which queries the public FuelEconomy.gov API.
